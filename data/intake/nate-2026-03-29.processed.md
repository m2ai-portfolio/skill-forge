**Post Title:** Executive Briefing: 33% of the world's helium supply just went offline. Your AI infrastructure plan doesn't account for it.
**Published:** Sun, 29 Mar 2026 15:02:24 GMT

## TLDR

The largest AI infrastructure buildout in history ($1.15T hyperscaler capex through 2027) has an unpriced dependency on helium -- a noble gas with no substitutes in semiconductor fabrication -- and one-third of global supply just went offline after Iranian missile strikes on a Qatari industrial complex. The briefing maps three transmission channels (helium supply, LNG energy costs, geopolitical restructuring) from the disruption to GPU cluster delivery timelines, arguing that infrastructure planning that ignores physical substrate risk is incomplete.
